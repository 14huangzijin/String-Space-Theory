**The Breaking of Entanglement**

Entanglement is extremely fragile.

When a measuring device probes one of the entangled particles, the device must emit a photon or other energy carrier toward the particle. This act itself is a local disturbance to the state of the string-web.

Therefore, what you measure is correlation, not communication. Information has never been transmitted faster than light.

**3. Double-Slit Interference and Wave-Particle Duality**

The double-slit experiment is the most famous experiment in quantum mechanics, and the most concentrated demonstration of wave-particle duality.

Electrons are emitted individually, one by one. After passing through two slits and accumulating countless electrons, alternating bright and dark interference fringes appear on the screen—as if each electron passed through both slits simultaneously and interfered with itself.

Quantum mechanics explains this using wave function superposition and measurement collapse, meaning the particle is a wave when unobserved, hence the self-interference.

String-Space Theory's answer is: the electron passes through only one slit.

But the electron is not bare. As it moves within the string-web, it excites an accompanying string-wave. This wave passes through both slits simultaneously, interferes, and then guides the electron's landing point.

A loop is a locked twist structure with a fixed geometric shape. The chaotic tremors that strike the loop's surface are rebounded by its geometric structure.

The rebounded tremors spread outward from the loop as a center, transforming from chaotic noise into regular waves. This is the string-wave.

It is like a wooden post in a lake. You see the post emanating circular ripples, but the post is not moving. In reality, these ripples are water waves formed by the rebounding of the water surface's micro-tremors against the post.
