
We now know that the string-web is space. So, what are matter and energy?

According to Einstein's mass-energy equation E=mc², energy and matter can be transformed into one another. This means they must be different manifestation states of the same entity.

Let us first look at energy. Energy is simply the bending of a string.

Because the string has elasticity, it naturally wants to maintain a straight line—its lowest energy state. When you bend it, it stores elastic potential energy, a force that wants to restore its original shape. This potential is energy. The more severe the bend, the more energy is stored; when the string returns to straight, the energy is released.

So, how does released energy travel along the string? It runs along the string in the form of a wave. The propagation speed of this vibration is what we commonly call the speed of light.

This is why the speed of light has an upper limit. The elastic property of the string is constant. Like a rubber band, whose material determines the maximum speed at which sound travels through it, the deformation and recovery speed of the string is constant. This is the fundamental reason why the speed of light is constant and unsurpassable. The speed of light is simply a "material property" of the string.

Now that we understand energy, let us look at matter.

If energy is a free, running "bend," then what is matter? The answer: matter is a bend locked dead onto the string.

So, how is this energy "locked" onto the string? Through geometric structure.

If possible, please find a piece of rope now—a stiff, elastic one, like a wire with a copper core. Let us create matter with our own hands.

Bending is energy.

Hold the two ends of the rope with your left and right hands. Keep your left hand fixed, and use your right hand to start twisting in one direction, applying a rotational force. Twist it like you are twisting a pretzel. If your rope is stiff and elastic enough, the moment you complete a full 360-degree twist, something magical happens: a perfect loop will spontaneously pop out on the rope.

(The picture: A topological soliton. It looks remarkably simple.)

In mathematics, this loop is called a topological soliton. Now, instead of twisting it, pull hard on both ends of the rope. You will find that no matter how much force you apply, you cannot make the loop disappear just by pulling the ends. (Provided your rope is not too soft.)

Congratulations, you have just created an "electron" with your own hands. That sturdy, circular loop twisted into your rope is the true physical structure of an electron.

Protected by its topological geometric structure, once the rope is twisted 360 degrees into a loop, it can never be pulled back into a straight line by tension on its ends. Even if the string's own elasticity pulls fiercely from both ends, once formed, this loop will persist forever. It can never be undone unless a force with the exact opposite twist direction acts upon it.

So, matter is twist-force locked inside a topological soliton on a string. Matter is energy in a locked state.

**The Motion of Matter: Inertia, Acceleration, and Jumping**

We have created matter. Now, how does it move within the string-web? This brings us to two crucial concepts in physics: inertia and acceleration.

Can a topological loop move along a string?

Of course, it can.

If you put your finger into the rope's loop and pull it, you will find the loop moves along the rope, and its topological structure does not change in the slightest.

You can think of the loop's movement like an ocean wave. A wave is not a substance, but a state. As a wave advances, what actually happens is that the water surface rises in successive locations.

Matter is not a substance; it is a state of the string. The movement of matter is simply different regions of the string changing into the shape of a loop.

Inertia:

When our "electron loop" glides smoothly along the string—performing what we call inertial motion—a dynamic cycle is actually running continuously inside it: its front end constantly "twists" the string into part of the loop, which requires energy.

At the same time, its back end is constantly "un-twisting," releasing the loop's string segment back into a straight line, which releases an equal amount of energy.

During inertial motion, the energy consumed at the loop's front end and the energy released at its back end are perfectly equal—a balanced budget. Therefore, it requires no external energy and can move perpetually without any external interference.

This is the true origin of inertia—it is not an inherent "laziness" of objects, but the inevitable result of the energy balance within the loop's "twist/un-twist" cycle.

Acceleration:

So, how do we accelerate this loop? It is simple: break the balance. To make it run faster, simply apply a faster twist-force to the back end of the loop.

This extra force will increase the twist speed at the front end, thereby increasing the twist and un-twist speed of the entire loop.

Conversely, to decelerate, apply a reverse twist-force to the front end, forcing it to slow down.

Once it comes to a stop, this decelerating twist-force will cause it to accelerate and move in the opposite direction.

So, acceleration and deceleration are fundamentally about using an external force to alter the operating rhythm of the loop's precise "twist/un-twist" dynamic system.

This elegant mechanism also fundamentally explains why no object with mass can ever reach or exceed the speed of light.

The loop's motion depends on the "twist-force" propagating along the string. And we know the maximum propagation speed of this force is exactly the "speed of light," determined by the string's elasticity.

Therefore, you can never use a pushing force—whose own maximum speed is the speed of light—to accelerate a loop beyond the speed of light. The speed limit of the medium is the speed limit of the object being carried.

And can a topological soliton jump between different strings? Absolutely. Because a topological soliton is, in essence, a twist-force. When it encounters a perpendicular string, this twist-force can act upon that perpendicular string, twisting it up. It looks exactly like the material loop has jumped onto the perpendicular string and continues its motion.

**Matter, Antimatter, and Annihilation**

Now, let us look at how this material loop is born on an infinitely long string in the universe.

Imagine a powerful twist-force suddenly striking one point on an infinitely long string.

This force does not stay at that point; it is conducted simultaneously toward both ends of the string. If the twist-force is fast and strong enough, two topological solitons will be born almost simultaneously on the left and right ends of the string.

(The picture: Symmetrically bulging matter and antimatter loops)

Amazingly, of these two topological solitons, if one bulges upwards, the other must dent downwards. If one is twisted clockwise, the other must be twisted counter-clockwise. They are like mirror images, with all properties exactly opposite.

Indeed, these two loops—one up, one down, with opposite rotation directions—are matter and antimatter.

Because that initial twist-force was conducted symmetrically toward the left and right, the topological solitons generated at the two ends must store twist-forces in completely opposite directions.

Now, let us employ the string's "elasticity" once more. Please pull on both ends of the rope simultaneously, bringing the two loops closer until they touch. You will see that the moment they meet, they instantly undo each other's twist, then both vanish, dissolving into a brief flutter.

This is matter-antimatter annihilation.

We already know that a topological soliton is not afraid of tension on its ends, but it fears an equal and opposite twist-force. This pair of symmetrically born topological solitons just happens to store twist-forces in completely opposite directions. As soon as they make contact, the two opposite forces instantly cancel each other out. The energy that was locked within the topological structure regains its freedom, transforming back into a running bend on the string—an energy wave propagating at the speed of light.

Thus, the process of matter-antimatter annihilation is the process of the twist-energy imprisoned in two topological solitons being re-released as free energy.

Any act of twisting will inevitably and symmetrically generate two topological loops of equal twist magnitude but opposite direction on the two ends of the twist point.

Once this pair of loops meets, they are fated to annihilate each other and transform back into energy. This is why, in nature, when energy transforms into matter, it always appears in matter-antimatter pairs, and why they rapidly annihilate each other immediately after birth, turning back into energy.

The properties of the string dictate the symmetry of motion.
