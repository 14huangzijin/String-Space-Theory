
Why does matter exist?

I am not asking about the form of matter's existence—we established from the very beginning that matter is energy locked within a topological loop.

The real question is this: matter is generated symmetrically, and then annihilates symmetrically. Even if a pair does not immediately annihilate at birth, they will cancel each other out upon encountering a substance with the opposite twist direction.

So, why does such a vast amount of matter still exist in our universe?

The Diluting Universe theory gives a clue: a black hole itself possesses a twist direction, so the matter produced by a black hole's explosion also has a twist direction.

But according to the determinism of String-Space Theory, no truly asymmetric structure exists in the universe. All matter exists symmetrically. The total twist of String-Space is zero.

Symmetry breaking does not exist—not symmetry within the diluting universe, but symmetry across the entire Greater Universe.

This means black holes are also generated in pairs and grow symmetrically. Our Mother Black Hole was not an isolated entity. It had a Mother Black Hole perfectly symmetric to it.

A pair of opposite Mother Black Holes formed symmetrically, moved away from each other along perfectly symmetric trajectories, grew symmetrically over billions of years, and finally exploded symmetrically, giving birth to two symmetric diluting universes—our universe, and its mirror image.

This is the Symmetric Universe Theory: there exists a universe completely identical to ours, yet completely symmetric.

All matter in that universe has the opposite twist direction to our matter. Humans also exist in that universe, except they are perfectly symmetric to us.

They too discovered the electron and established physics. They too believe their own universe is made of matter. At this very moment, a symmetric you, in that distant symmetric universe, is likewise reading this very passage of text.

Imagine a scientist in our universe who creates a pair of matter and antimatter in a laboratory and then breaks their symmetric state. He would think he has truly violated the symmetry of matter.

But at the same instant, a symmetric scientist in the symmetric universe, in a symmetric laboratory, also creates a pair of matter and antimatter, and likewise breaks their symmetric state. He too thinks he has violated the symmetry.

But he does not realize that he thinks he is observing symmetry, while he himself is a part of the symmetric motion. Symmetry can never be broken.

This is an inevitable deduction of String-Space Theory—no superposition states exist. Everything is deterministic. Everything is also symmetric. The total twist of String-Space is zero.

Now, suppose you are the first astronaut to fly out of the diluting universe. You board a near-light-speed spacecraft, cross the boundary of the dilution bubble, and enter the Greater Universe.

Do not worry. It is very safe here. The vast majority of matter in the Greater Universe is dark matter and black holes. They will not mutually annihilate with you. Dark matter will pass directly through your body. Black holes can be circumvented by observing the density of the string-web.

If your trajectory is correct and you do not lose your way, you will fly all the way toward the symmetric dilution bubble.

At the central point between the two symmetric universes—which is also the central symmetry point of the entire String-Space—you see a spacecraft identical to your own.

However, you are not standing face to face. Your forward-backward, left-right, and up-down orientations are all opposite along the central point.

Forward-backward are opposite, so you can face each other. But up-down are also opposite, so the person you see is upside down.

You instinctively want to flip your body, to be head-to-head and foot-to-foot with the other you.

But at the same time, the other you also flips symmetrically. You immediately stop. The other, like a person in a mirror, also stops simultaneously.

This is all like looking into a mirror. But a mirror only has left-right symmetry. The person across from you is not only left-right reversed, but also up-down reversed.

If you try to make a movement different from the other, you will find yourself like a child trying to be faster than their reflection, or trying to make a different movement—everything is futile.

No sound can be transmitted in space. You cannot touch each other either—after all, you are composed of matter and antimatter. The moment you touch, you will mutually annihilate.

But as long as you keep your distance, it is safe. You try many times, and finally have to admit that your movements can never produce any asymmetry.

You begin to think: what if there were a planet at the symmetry point? Could you calibrate your standing orientation upon it, head-to-head and foot-to-foot, and break this symmetry?

But along the way here, you did not see many celestial bodies in String-Space.

Everything is symmetric. It is simply symmetry in three dimensions.

You are tired. There is no need to try and make a different movement from the mirror.

This is the symmetric universe. A universe perfectly symmetric to our own.

At this very moment, there is a person identical to you, in that distant symmetric universe, reading this very passage of text, turning thoughts identical to yours in his mind.
