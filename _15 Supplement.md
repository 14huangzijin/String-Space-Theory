**1. A Black Hole Never Stops Rotating**

Every black hole must be rotating. This is because the vortex zone, a constituent part of a black hole, is itself a twisting twist-pair.

The larger the black hole, the faster its rotation, because a larger vortex zone means faster rotation.

When a black hole's rotation slows, its event horizon is shrinking. The moment its rotation stops is the moment its event horizon vanishes completely, leaving only the central singularity.

In the next instant, the black hole transforms into a white hole.

Therefore, every black hole—every singularity possessing an event horizon—will rotate continuously.

The rotation speed is proportional to the size of the event horizon. The accretion disk is likewise proportional to the event horizon.
This is because the accretion disk results from the black hole being unable to swallow its food. If a black hole is not overfed, it cannot have an accretion disk.

You can never find a non-rotating black hole. This is a prediction of String-Space.

**2. Neutrino Oscillation**

In standard physics, neutrino oscillation means that a tau neutrino, running through the vacuum, gradually turns into a muon neutrino, and a muon neutrino gradually turns into an electron neutrino.

In String-Space Theory, this process has a single cause: the more perpendicular intersection points a spiral pulse passes through, the flatter it is pressed.

The "flavor" of a neutrino is not, in fact, classified by energy level, but by whether it can generate a specific material loop.

A spiral pulse can carry a great deal of total energy. But if it has run too long and been pressed too flat, it becomes very difficult for the trailing loop to catch up to the leading loop. When the Tornado Effect takes place, because the waveform has been flattened, the front end of the tornado stretches very long before the trailing loop finally catches on. A large amount of the tornado at the front escapes. The twist-force remaining to close into a material loop is very little.

Thus, a spiral pulse may possess the total energy to generate a tau, but because its waveform is too flat, it can only generate a muon in the end. This spiral pulse is then called a muon antineutrino.

This is what is meant by a neutrino changing flavor. In truth, the total energy of the spiral pulse has not diminished. But what it generates in the end is not a tau, but a muon. So from the perspective of standard physics, it has turned into a muon antineutrino.

Where did those escaped tornado pulses go? Are they still spiral pulses?

In String-Space Theory, they are of course still spiral pulses.
But in standard physics, that segment of dissipated energy, which can no longer form any particle, is called vacuum zero-point energy.

So, the essence of so-called neutrino oscillation is this:

A tau pulse, pressed flat by perpendicular intersection points, becomes a muon pulse capable only of generating a muon. Running further, pressed flatter still, it becomes an electron pulse capable only of generating an electron.

And even further? The electron pulse is pressed even flatter, incapable of generating even a single electron. In the detectors of standard physics, it "vanishes," relegated to the category of vacuum zero-point energy.

This explains the famous mystery of the "missing solar neutrinos"—the observed value of electron neutrinos coming from the Sun is only one-third of the theoretical prediction.
The reason is that the remaining two-thirds, having run long enough within the string-web, were pressed too flat, and in the end could not even twist out an electron. Standard physics failed to recognize them and took them for vacuum zero-point energy.

**3. Virtual Particle Pairs**

We already know that the longer a spiral pulse runs and the flatter its waveform becomes, the lighter the material loop it can generate.
So, what happens when two spiral pulses that have both run very flat and possess extremely low energy collide head-on within the string-web?

The answer is a virtual particle pair.

Two very weak spiral pulses collide head-on. Their twist-forces cancel each other out, transforming into symmetric wave oscillation, which then acts upon a perpendicular string. The perpendicular string emits two new spiral pulses. The Tornado Effect initiates, the trailing loop catches the leading loop, and a material loop appears.

However, because the newly generated spiral pulses are also very weak, although a loop has formed, its internal twist degree is less than 360 degrees. At this moment, this topological loop is a virtual particle.

A so-called virtual particle pair is a topological loop that has formed a ring shape but has not reached a full 360 degrees of twist.

Because it has not reached 360 degrees, the topological structure does not protect the virtual particle. The virtual particle loops do not need to annihilate each other; they are directly flattened by the elastic tension of the string, dissolving back into a straight line. This is the same as the escaped tornado pulses in neutrino oscillation—they all ultimately return to that ocean of extremely low-energy spiral pulses.

Scientists have discovered that if a newly formed virtual particle pair is forcibly pulled apart, the virtual particles do not stably exist like real particles (i.e., ordinary topological loops). Although the two opposite virtual particles have not touched each other, they each vanish on their own.

The String-Space explanation is: the twist degree of a virtual particle is less than 360 degrees, so it does not require annihilation. The string's tension will naturally pull it apart, releasing the spiral pulse inside.

Scientists have also discovered that virtual particles do not emit light waves when they vanish, but they do release energy. They call this vacuum zero-point energy. But in String-Space, this thing is called a spiral pulse.

Because the two virtual particles do not mutually annihilate, but are each flattened by the string, what they release can certainly not be light waves, but rather the twist-force they themselves carry—that is, a faint spiral pulse.

Standard physics holds that vacuum zero-point energy exists within the vacuum, that vacuum zero-point energy becomes virtual particles, and that the virtual particles then become vacuum zero-point energy once more. But String-Space holds that vacuum zero-point energy is simply spiral pulses. It is just that standard physics measures neutrinos according to energy levels, and the lowest level of neutrino is the electron neutrino. So, spiral pulses with energy lower than an electron neutrino went unrecognized by them, and they consequently called it vacuum zero-point energy.

I regret to say that, according to the deductions of String-Space Theory, Hawking radiation does not exist.

This is because a virtual particle pair does not have one particle fall into the black hole while the other escapes at the speed of light, carrying away the black hole's energy. A black hole will not evaporate because of virtual particle pairs. Hawking radiation does not exist.
